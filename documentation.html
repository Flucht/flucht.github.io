Documentation

1. Introduction

In this page you can find all the documentation about the project Flucht. Flucht explores the global phenomenon of immigration in terms of economic and political factors, in order to understand which one of them has a major impact on the phenomenon of immigration itself.
You can find all the scripts of our data in the GitHub page of the project.

2. Scenario

In order to accomplish our research case, we collected data from different sources and re-used it to create our own dataset. We aimed at re-using datasets free of cognitive biases, prejudices and discriminations, fair and reliable, legally valid, relevant, consistent and accurate. Due to the variety of institutional sources we used this was not easy task. During the research and management of data we encountered some difficulties due mainly to legal and accessibility issues. The results of our research provide with a global economic and political perspective towards to phenomenon of immigration, hopefully to be object of interest in the public domain.

3. Datasets

3.1. Original Datasets

The datasets used to cover the economic and political aspects affecting immigration include the following data from the respective sources:

  • Constant GDP per Capita. Source: The World Bank Group
  • GDP per Capita Growth. Source: The World Bank Group.
  • Unemployment ILO Rate. Source: The World Bank Group.
  • Unemployment National Rate. Source: The World Bank Group.
  • Constant Income per Capita. Source: The World Bank Group.
  • Income per Capita Growth. Source: The World Bank Group.
  • Terrorism. Source: National Consortium for the Study of Terrorism and Responses to Terrorism (START) @ University of Maryland.
  • War and other violent events. Source: UCDP (Uppsala Conflict Data Program).
  • Quality of democracy. Source: The Economist Intelligence Unit.
  • Demographics of refugees, their origins and their current residence. Source: UNHCR.
